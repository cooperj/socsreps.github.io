Frequently Asked Questions

The course reps get asked many questions and some of them require the same support. This can include things such as sign posting, inquiring on ways to get help or even how to give feedback about a lecture. To help with this we have put together some Useful Links and FAQs. If you want to add an FAQ or a new Useful Link please contact any one of us through the email links on our Contact Us page or head over to our GitHub repo and make a pull request or raise an issue. All emails are received in the strictest of confidence.

The best and quickest way is to send an email to Khaled Bachour (kbachour@lincoln.ac.uk) who is the Senior Tutor within SoCS. Khaled Bachour leads the SoCS personal tutor program and can help you make contact with your personal tutor and is also the contact point if you have any personal tutor issues.

There are several ways to contact your course reps but the best way is via email. All course reps emails can be found in the Contact Us section of the website which is found at the top of every web page. Alternately, all rep contact details are on the LincolnSU website.

The School Rep creates a monthly report which includes all the activities that the school and course reps have been up to. This report is available to all students on the LincolnSU website and is completed by the 3rd Wednesday of every month. Every report is archived and located at www.lincolnsu.com/officer-reports. Click on the Archive tab to see all past reports. Additionally, for current information this SoCS reps blog is also a useful place to catch up or contact your reps who will always be very happy to answer any questions. No idea or concern is too small.

The best first contact is the module coordinator. However, the course reps are able to represent any concern you have on your behalf to the school or module coordinators but this should ideally be done if you are not happy with your previous contact. A good way to let us know of an idea is also via www.lincolnsu.com/course-mate. Here you can formally (through the SU website) submit any feedback about your course and the reps will pick this up via the SU.

The course reps are not directly able to help you with any course work or assignments. However, they are able to give you ideas of who best to contact for any help. Often, a quick discussion with the module coordinators can help clarify any problems.
