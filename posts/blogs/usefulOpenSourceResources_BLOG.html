Useful Open Source Resources

By Garry Clawson

October 15, 2020

When I first started University I promised myself all the best equipment on the false belief that this would give me the highest chances of being successful. Under that false influence and on that basis, ... I bought a Mac. A top of the range, 27" 5K screen, all the bells and whistles, Mac. It looks beautiful but as I slowly get to know it, I have found that the beauty is only skin deep. Doing this put me firmly in the bracket of those who have 'all the gear, but no idea'.

Since then I have tried to improve my understanding and have begun to embrace open source hardware and software and I was lucky enough to attend the RISC-V Roadshow in London and the Embedded Linux Conference in Lyon, France.

Although the journey from something like a Mac and its eco-system to completely open source software, or Free and Open source software can take some doing, I am enjoying it so far. I do sometimes fall back on things that are simpler or have a nicer interface but that is just part of the transition, but also part of the challenge, as it is key to give back to the open source community, by making suggestions and developing improvements. I am by no means there yet, but I wanted to share with you a non exhaustive list of open source software that may interest you or you may find useful through your University career and beyond. This is not something that I have collated but is something that I have found from various threads across the interweb.

I hope you find this list useful. The links are in no order and by no means complete, so if you see something that would be useful then please head over to our GitHub repo and make a pull request or raise an issue. Alternatively you can contact any one of us through our email or anonymously by using our Feedback page. All emails are received in the strictest of confidence.
